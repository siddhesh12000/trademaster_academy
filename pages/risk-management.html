Module 7

Risk Management

The holy grail of trading. Learn to protect your capital, size your positions correctly, and ensure long-term survival.

The Most Important Module

You can have a mediocre trading strategy and still be profitable with excellent risk management. However, you can have the best strategy in the world and blow your account without it.

Rule #1: Capital Preservation

Your primary job as a trader is not to make money, it is to protect the money you already have. If you lose your capital, you are out of business.

Core Concepts

Risk-to-Reward Ratio (R:R)

This measures how much you are risking on a trade compared to how much you expect to gain.

  • If you risk $100 to make $200, your R:R is 1:2.
  • With a 1:2 R:R, you only need a 33% win rate to break even. A 40% win rate makes you highly profitable.

Position Sizing (The 1% Rule)

Never risk more than 1% to 2% of your total account capital on a single trade. This prevents emotional decision-making and protects you from a string of inevitable losses (drawdowns).

Position Size Formula

Position Size = (Account Balance × Risk %) / Stop Loss (in points/pips/dollars)

Example: $10,000 account, risking 1% ($100). If your Stop Loss is 20 pips away, your position size is calculated based on that $100 maximum loss.

Win Rate vs. Expectancy

Win rate is meaningless without considering R:R. Expectancy is what matters.

Expectancy = (Win Rate × Average Win) - (Loss Rate × Average Loss)

Trader A

  • Win Rate: 80%
  • Average Win: $100
  • Average Loss: -$500
  • Negative Expectancy (Losing Trader)

Trader B

  • Win Rate: 40%
  • Average Win: $300
  • Average Loss: -$100
  • Positive Expectancy (Profitable Trader)

Trade Management Strategies

Stop Loss Strategies

  • Hard Stop: Placed in the market system, executed automatically. (Recommended)
  • Mental Stop: Kept in your head. Highly dangerous for beginners due to emotional attachment.
  • Trailing Stop: Moving your stop loss into profit as the trade goes in your favor. Locks in gains.

Taking Partial Profits

Taking partials means closing a portion of your position (e.g., 50%) when it reaches an initial target, and moving the stop loss to breakeven for the remainder. This ensures you secure some profit and creates a "risk-free" trade for the runner.
