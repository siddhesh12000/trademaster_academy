Module 3

Smart Money Concepts

Understand how institutions trade, identify footprints, and align your trades with the smart money.

Introduction to SMC

Smart Money Concepts (SMC) refers to the trading methodology that attempts to track the actions of institutional participants, central banks, and market makers. Instead of retail patterns, SMC focuses on liquidity, order flow, and market structure.

Core Principle

The market is a mechanism for delivering price from one pool of liquidity to another. Institutions require massive liquidity to enter and exit their positions without causing extreme slippage.

Market Structure Basics

Understanding market structure is the foundation of SMC. It dictates the current trend and potential reversal points.

Uptrend Structure

  • HH: Higher High
  • HL: Higher Low

Downtrend Structure

  • LH: Lower High
  • LL: Lower Low

Break of Structure (BOS)

A BOS occurs when price breaks and closes past a previous structural high (in an uptrend) or low (in a downtrend), confirming the continuation of the trend.

Change of Character (CHOCH)

A CHOCH indicates a potential trend reversal. It happens when price fails to make a new HH/LL and instead breaks the previous structural HL/LH.

Understanding Liquidity

Liquidity refers to areas on the chart where a large number of orders (stop losses, buy stops, sell stops) rest. Smart money targets these areas to build their positions.

  • Equal Highs (EQH) & Equal Lows (EQL): Retail traders view these as strong support/resistance, placing stop losses just beyond them. SMC views them as prime targets for liquidity sweeps.
  • Inducement (IDM): Minor structure highs/lows that induce early buyers/sellers before the true move occurs.

Institutional Footprints

Order Block (OB)

An Order Block is the last consecutive bearish candle(s) before a strong bullish impulse (Bullish OB), or the last bullish candle(s) before a strong bearish drop (Bearish OB). It represents where institutions placed massive orders.

Fair Value Gap (FVG)

An FVG (or Imbalance) occurs when price moves so rapidly that liquidity is only offered on one side of the market. It is a three-candle formation where the wicks of the 1st and 3rd candles do not overlap, leaving a gap in the 2nd candle.

Breaker Block & Mitigation Block

Breaker Block: A failed Order Block that resulted in a liquidity sweep (making a new high/low) before the trend reversed. Price often returns to test it from the other side.

Mitigation Block: Similar to a Breaker, but it occurs without a prior liquidity sweep (fails to make a new high/low).

Premium, Discount & OTE

Institutions prefer to buy at a discount and sell at a premium.

  • Premium: Above the 50% equilibrium level of a price range. Ideal for finding short setups.
  • Discount: Below the 50% equilibrium level. Ideal for finding long setups.
  • Optimal Trade Entry (OTE): The "sweet spot" within the Fibonacci retracement, typically between the 61.8% and 78.6% levels.

Summary

SMC is about reading the narrative of price. Identify the higher timeframe trend, spot where retail liquidity lies, wait for a sweep of that liquidity into an institutional POI (Order Block, FVG), and look for a lower timeframe CHOCH for entry.
