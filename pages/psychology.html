Module 8

Trading Psychology

Master your emotions. Learn to overcome fear, greed, and FOMO to execute your edge flawlessly.

The Inner Game of Trading

Trading is 20% strategy and 80% psychology. The market is a mirror that reflects your deepest fears and insecurities. Success comes not from predicting the market, but from managing yourself.

Complete Acceptance of Risk

Before you click the buy or sell button, you must mentally accept that the money risked on the trade is already gone. If you are anxious while in a trade, your position size is too large or you haven't truly accepted the risk.

The 4 Horsemen of Trading Psychology

FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out)

Entering a trade late because price is moving fast and you want to be part of it. Usually results in buying the top or selling the bottom.

Cure: Accept that the market provides endless opportunities.

Greed

Holding a winning trade too long hoping for a home run, or increasing risk excessively after a winning streak.

Cure: Set mechanical take-profit targets and stick to them.

Fear

Hesitating to take a valid setup because of recent losses, or closing a trade prematurely before it reaches its target.

Cure: Trade smaller sizes to reduce emotional attachment.

Revenge Trading

Taking an impulsive, unplanned trade immediately after a loss to "win back" the money. This often leads to account blowouts.

Cure: Walk away from the screen after any loss.

Overtrading

Overtrading is taking setups that don't meet your strict criteria just because you are bored or feel the need to be "in the market." Cash is a valid position. Sometimes the best trade is no trade.

The Trading Plan

A trading plan is your business plan. It removes in-the-moment decision making. It should dictate:

  • What specific setups you trade (Your Edge)
  • Which markets and timeframes you trade
  • Your exact risk per trade (e.g., 1%)
  • Your max daily loss limit (e.g., stop trading for the day if down 2%)

Mark Douglas on Consistency:

"If you want to create consistent results, you must trade from a completely objective state of mind, which means you have to trade without fear."
