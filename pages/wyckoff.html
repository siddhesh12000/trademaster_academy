Wyckoff Theory

Master the market cycle, accumulation/distribution schematics, and the motives of the "Composite Man".

Introduction to Wyckoff

Richard Wyckoff was a pioneer of early 20th-century technical analysis. His method involves analyzing price action, volume, and time to determine the intentions of the "Smart Money" or what he called the Composite Man.

The Composite Man

Wyckoff proposed a heuristic to imagine that all the buying and selling in the market is the work of one single entity: The Composite Man. He manipulates the market to his advantage. If you understand his motives (accumulating low to sell high, or distributing high to buy low), you can trade alongside him.

The Market Cycle

According to Wyckoff, the market can be understood and anticipated through a continuous four-stage cycle:

1. Accumulation

The Composite Man quietly buys (accumulates) assets from retail traders at low prices after a markdown.

2. Markup

Once accumulation is complete, the lack of supply causes prices to trend upwards easily.

3. Distribution

The Composite Man carefully sells (distributes) his accumulated positions to retail buyers at high prices.

4. Markdown

With demand exhausted, the market trends downwards, seeking the next area of accumulation.

Wyckoff Schematics & Phases

Both Accumulation and Distribution events occur within trading ranges and are broken down into five distinct phases (A through E).

Phases of Accumulation

  • Phase A (Stopping the Downtrend): Exhaustion of previous selling. Key events include Preliminary Support (PS), Selling Climax (SC), Automatic Rally (AR), and Secondary Test (ST).
  • Phase B (Building a Cause): The longest phase where the Composite Man accumulates. Price swings up and down within the range, creating "cause" for the next move.
  • Phase C (Testing): The defining moment. Price often breaks below the range support (a "Spring" or "Shakeout") to trap early sellers and collect the final liquidity before the markup.
  • Phase D (Trend Inside the Range): Price shows consistent strength, making Higher Highs and Higher Lows, breaking the range resistance with strong volume (Sign of Strength - SOS).
  • Phase E (Trend Outside the Range): The markdown is over; the markup begins. Price leaves the accumulation range completely.

Wyckoff Accumulation Schematic

Springs and Upthrusts

These are the hallmark signatures of Phase C. They are false breakouts designed to stop out retail traders and grab liquidity.

  • Spring (Accumulation): Price dips below the trading range support, stops out buyers, triggers breakdown sellers, and then rapidly reverses back into the range.
  • Upthrust (Distribution): Price spikes above the trading range resistance, stops out sellers, triggers breakout buyers, and then rapidly reverses downwards.
