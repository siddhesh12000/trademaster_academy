Price Action Mastery

Learn to read the raw footprint of the market through candlesticks, levels, and patterns without indicators.

What is Price Action?

Price action trading is the discipline of making all trading decisions from a stripped-down or "naked" price chart. It focuses entirely on the behavior of price itself, avoiding lagging indicators.

Candlestick Anatomy

Candlesticks represent the battle between buyers (bulls) and sellers (bears) over a specific time period.

Essential Candlestick Patterns

Engulfing

A large candle that completely eclipses the previous smaller candle. Strong reversal signal.

Pin Bar (Hammer/Shooting Star)

A small body with a very long wick, showing severe rejection of a price level.

Doji

Open and close are virtually identical. Indicates extreme indecision in the market.

Support, Resistance & Trendlines

These are the foundational barriers where price has historically had difficulty passing.

Support & Resistance

Support: A "floor" where buying interest is strong enough to overcome selling pressure.

Resistance: A "ceiling" where selling pressure overcomes buying interest.

Trendlines

Diagonal lines drawn connecting higher lows (uptrend) or lower highs (downtrend). A valid trendline needs at least three touches.

Market Dynamics: Breakouts, Retests & Fakeouts

  • Breakout: Price confidently closes past a significant support/resistance level with momentum.
  • Retest / Pullback: After a breakout, price often returns to test the broken level (which swaps from resistance to support, or vice versa).
  • Fakeout (False Breakout): Price pierces a level to trap breakout traders, then immediately reverses back into the range.
