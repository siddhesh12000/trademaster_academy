Inner Circle Trader (ICT) Concepts

Master algorithmic price delivery, time and price theory, and high-probability setups like the Silver Bullet.

Introduction to ICT

ICT (Inner Circle Trader) concepts are based on the premise that financial markets are controlled by an algorithm (IPDA - Interbank Price Delivery Algorithm). It heavily emphasizes the relationship between Time and Price, suggesting that specific setups only occur at specific times of the day.

Time and Price Theory

Time is the most critical factor in ICT. If you have the right price level but the wrong time, the trade setup is invalid.

Kill Zones

Specific time windows where highest volatility and manipulation occur. (e.g., London Open, NY Open).

Silver Bullet

A specific 1-hour window (e.g., 10AM-11AM EST) designed to offer at least one high probability setup.

Daily Bias

The anticipated directional move for the day, determined by higher timeframe Market Maker Models.

Power of Three (AMD) & Judas Swing

AMD Concept

Accumulation: Smart money builds positions in consolidation (often Asian session).

Manipulation (Judas Swing): A fake move opposite to the true daily bias to trap retail traders (often London session).

Distribution: The true expansion move in the direction of the daily bias (often NY session).

Illustration: The Power of Three (Bullish Day Example)

PD Arrays (Premium/Discount Arrays)

PD Arrays are the specific institutional reference points ICT uses to enter trades. They are ranked by importance.

  • Old Highs / Old Lows: The most significant pools of liquidity.
  • Rejection Blocks: Wicks that sweep liquidity.
  • Order Blocks: Discussed in SMC.
  • Fair Value Gaps (FVG) / Imbalances: Areas where price delivered inefficiently.
  • Breaker Blocks / Mitigation Blocks: Failed order blocks.

SMT Divergence (Smart Money Tool)

SMT Divergence is used to confirm accumulation or distribution by comparing closely correlated assets (e.g., EURUSD and GBPUSD, or NQ and ES).

If EURUSD makes a Higher High, but GBPUSD fails to make a Higher High and makes a Lower High instead, that crack in correlation is an SMT Divergence. It signals underlying institutional weakness or strength, often preceding a reversal.

Market Maker Models (MMXM)

The Market Maker Buy Model (MMBM) and Market Maker Sell Model (MMSM) outline the complete cycle of price delivery from consolidation, out to a higher timeframe POI, and reversing back to the original consolidation.
